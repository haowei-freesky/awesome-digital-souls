## What Is This 🧠

`Digital souls` refers to those human traces that can be recorded, distilled, transferred, and reused: ways of speaking, habits of judgment, professional experience, emotional responses, fragments of memory, relationship roles, and even that very specific feeling of "what would this person do here?" It is not about reducing a person to data, but about leaving behind an interface through which human experience can remain interactive, extendable, and callable again.

`Skills distillation` means breaking down abilities that originally lived only inside a specific person and condensing them into reusable AI assets, such as a `skill`, `agent`, `persona`, workflow, or memory system. It is not about cloning a universal human being, but about extracting units of experience that are actually valuable: how someone communicates, how they comfort others, how they write a weekly report, how they mentor a student, or how they rescue a collapsing experiment late at night.

Around large models and skill distillation, two opposite emotions often exist at the same time:

> "You people building large models are traitors to code. You've already killed our frontend brothers, and now you're coming for backend brothers, testing brothers, ops brothers, infosec brothers, IC brothers, and in the end yourselves and all humanity."

> "You people building large models are basically gods of code. You've already liberated our frontend brothers, and now you'll liberate backend brothers, testing brothers, ops brothers, infosec brothers, IC brothers, and in the end yourselves and all humanity."

This repository would rather acknowledge that tension head-on, but its stance is also clear: we are **human-centered**. The goal of technology is not to replace the existence of human beings, but to serve them; not to flatten what makes people unique, but to preserve, amplify, and pass on the experience, personality, and connection that would otherwise fade with time.

So what this repository collects is not just project links, but also a set of questions:

- To what extent can a person's experience be distilled?
- In what form can the companionship within a relationship continue?
- How can a role's judgment and style be preserved with respect?
- When we build `digital souls`, how do we keep humans at the center from start to finish?

## How To Contribute 🤝

Contributions of more skill concepts or open-source projects in the "digital souls" direction are very welcome. Priority is given to:

- `skill` / `agent` projects with clear role settings and concrete use cases
- Projects that reflect the distillation of personality, memory, or professional experience
- Reproducible entries with traceable sources, ideally with a repository or demo

Suggested submission format:

```md
| `skill-name` | Role | Use Cases | Core Capabilities |
```
